Unveiling the Blaze: Top 5 Satellite Datasets for Wildfire Mapping (Inspired by Ankur Shah)

Wildfires, those terrifying spectacles of nature's raw power, are unfortunately becoming an increasingly common sight. Their ferocity and frequency demand better tools for monitoring and understanding their impact. Thankfully, we have a powerful ally orbiting above us: satellites.

Inspired by Ankur Shah's insightful LinkedIn post, I wanted to delve deeper into the satellite datasets that are revolutionizing wildfire monitoring. These datasets provide critical information, enabling us to track fires in near real-time, assess damage, and understand long-term trends.

Let's explore five key satellite datasets that are essential for wildfire mapping:

Satellite Datasets for Wildfire Monitoring

Dataset Resolution Update Frequency Best For
VIIRS ~375 m 6-hourly Near-real-time active fire detection, fire front tracking
MODIS 1 km (thermal) Daily Long-term fire trends, smoke tracking
GOES 2 km Every 5-10 minutes Real-time wildfire tracking, rapid fire spread detection
Sentinel-2 10 m (optical), 20 m (SWIR) 5 days Burned area mapping, vegetation recovery, detailed fire scars
Landsat-8/9 30 m (visible & SWIR), 100 m (thermal) 16 days Historical fire trends, detailed burn scars, vegetation impact
